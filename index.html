About Me

I am a PhD student in Statistics at Florida State University and a Graduate Teaching Assistant in the Department of Statistics. My research interests include high-dimensional statistics, regularization methods, statistical learning, feature stability, interpretable machine learning, and predictive modeling.

My work focuses on developing and evaluating statistical methods for variable selection, prediction, and inference, with applications in public health, education, biomedical research, and sports analytics.

Research Interests

  • High-dimensional statistical modeling
  • Regularization methods: ridge, LASSO, and elastic net
  • Feature stability and interpretability
  • Post-selection inference and uncertainty quantification
  • Statistical learning and predictive modeling
  • Applications in biomedical, education, public health, and sports analytics
