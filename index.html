About Me

I am a PhD student in Statistics at Florida State University. My research interests include high-dimensional statistics, regularization methods, statistical learning, feature stability, interpretable machine learning, and predictive modeling.

My work focuses on developing and evaluating statistical methods for variable selection, prediction, and inference, with applications in public health, education, biomedical research, and sports analytics.

Research Interests

  • High-Dimensional Statistical Modeling
  • Regularization Methods (Ridge, LASSO, Elastic Net)
  • Feature Stability and Interpretability
  • Post-Selection Inference
  • Statistical Learning and Prediction
  • Applications in Biomedical, Educational, and Public Health Data

Current Research Projects

  • FIFA World Cup Tournament Progression Prediction
    Penalized logistic regression and bootstrap stability selection for interpretable prediction.
  • Student Stress Prediction
    Stable predictor identification using penalized regression and machine learning models.
  • HDL Cholesterol Prediction
    Feature stability and interpretability using NHANES data.
  • Breast Cancer Diagnosis
    Penalized logistic regression for biomedical classification.
  • Revisiting Tibshirani's LASSO Examples
    Modern replication and extension of classic regularization studies.
