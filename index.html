About me

I am a graduate student and teaching assistant in Statistics at Florida State University. My academic interests are in high-dimensional statistics, regularization methods, statistical learning, and interpretable machine learning.

My research focuses on regularization, inference, and stability in high-dimensional statistical models, with applications to risk prediction, education data, biomedical data, and public health.

Research interests

  • High-dimensional statistical modeling
  • Regularization methods: ridge, lasso, and elastic net
  • Post-selection inference and uncertainty quantification
  • Feature stability and interpretability
  • Applications in biomedical, education, and public health data

Selected projects

  • Feature Stability and Interpretability in Penalized Logistic Regression
    Application to breast cancer diagnosis.
  • Regularized Logistic Regression for Student Academic Outcomes
    Comparing ridge, lasso, and elastic-net models for educational risk prediction.
  • Feature Stability in Predicting HDL Cholesterol
    An NHANES-based application using regularized regression and bootstrap stability.
