Curriculum Vitae

I am a PhD student in Statistics at Florida State University with research interests in high-dimensional statistics, statistical learning, penalized regression, variable selection, and interpretable machine learning. My work focuses on developing statistically rigorous and interpretable predictive models with applications in healthcare, education, sports analytics, and public policy.

Selected Research Projects

Interpretable Prediction of FIFA World Cup Tournament Progression

Developed interpretable predictive models using logistic regression, penalized regression, and bootstrap stability selection for forecasting FIFA World Cup progression.

Stable Predictor Identification for Student Stress

Compared penalized regression and machine learning methods to identify stable predictors of student stress using large educational datasets.

Revisiting Tibshirani (1996) with Modern Regularization Methods

Reproduced and extended classical LASSO simulations using contemporary regularization techniques and computational tools.

Feature Stability in High-Dimensional Classification

Research on sparse discriminant analysis, stability selection, and high-dimensional variable selection.
